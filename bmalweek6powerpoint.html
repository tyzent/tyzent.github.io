Quotes from references

Selfless Service

  • public faith in leadership has declined, indicating the need for a reevaluation of what makes a good leader.
  • selfless service to others and “emphasizes collective solidarity, considers moral behavior a duty, emphasizes interpersonal responsibilities”
  • other-orientation style that contributes to a Transformational leadership style with the potential to inspire others.

Courage

  • Sometimes it does look like the daring rescue, the selfless sacrifice, or the unimaginable feat—raw, spontaneous, and unyielding. We celebrate this kind of courage, as we should, but fail to recognize the many other ways that courage shows up in ourselves and others.
  • Author and poet David Whyte wrote that courage “is what love looks like when tested by the simple everyday necessities of being alive….only from the outside and only by looking back, does it look like courage.”
  • Maybe, as David Whyte suggests, courage is simply love in action.
  • Acting with courage does not imply the absence of fear. Perhaps claiming our courage is a form of birthing ourselves—a painful, joyful, messy affair with no certain outcomes, one that requires us to surrender but also to show up.

Integrity

  • Leader behavioral integrity (leader integrity), defined as an employee’s perception of a leader’s overall consistency between behavior and espoused values, has important organizational implications. It is a key predictor of individuals’ trust in, satisfaction with, and desire to work for their leaders.
  • It is also negatively related to employee expedience behaviors (cutting corners), absenteeism, and cynicism and is positively related to customer satisfaction and unit-level profitability
